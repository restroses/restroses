Auditing

Systematic examination and evaluation of an organization, process, system, or entity to ensure compliance with established standards, regulations, or practices. The primary objective of auditing is to provide an independent assessment of whether the subject being audited is operating effectively and efficiently, and if it adheres to applicable laws, regulations, policies, or industry standards.

Bookkeeping

Systematic recording, organizing, and storing of financial transactions and information related to a business or organization. It is a fundamental part of accounting and essential for maintaining accurate financial records.

Financing

Process of providing funds or capital for business operations, investments, projects, or personal expenditures. It involves acquiring money that is necessary to sustain or grow activities, typically through various sources such as loans, equity investments, grants, or other forms of credit.
